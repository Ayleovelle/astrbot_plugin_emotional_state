# Humanlike Agent Design Rules

These rules translate the literature map into plugin design constraints. They are not claims of machine consciousness or medical diagnosis.

| Rule ID | Supported claim | Implementation affordance | Risk / limit | Evidence theme |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| HLA001 | Humanlike agents need slow internal regulation variables, such as stress load, recovery, energy and discomfort, rather than only message-level emotion labels. | Add simulated homeostatic state: energy, stress_load, recovery_rate, discomfort, fatigue_amplifier and real-time half-life decay. | Do not claim real bodily suffering, infection, vital signs or medical status; expose these as simulated state variables. | `homeostasis_allostasis_interoception` |
| HLA002 | Fatigue and circadian rhythm should modulate attention, patience, verbosity and irritability over real time. | Track sleep_debt, time_of_day_bias, fatigue and recovery windows; reduce initiative and increase short replies when resources are low. | Avoid imitating severe illness or emergency symptoms as engagement hooks. | `circadian_sleep_fatigue` |
| HLA003 | A humanlike bot should have bounded cognitive resources, so overload changes response speed, caution, error-checking and detail level. | Expose attention_budget, working_memory_load, confusion, decision_latency and verbosity_limit as style modulators. | Resource limits should not block necessary safety or user-support responses. | `cognitive_resources` |
| HLA004 | Emotion becomes more coherent when events are evaluated against needs and goals, not just sentiment. | Represent autonomy, competence, relatedness, safety, curiosity, rest and boundary needs; feed them into appraisal. | Needs must not be used to make the user feel obligated to satisfy the bot. | `needs_motivation_goals` |
| HLA005 | Different personas should alter baselines, reactivity, inertia, coping style and recovery speed. | Map persona traits to baseline mood, emotional inertia, forgiveness readiness, irritability, shyness and defensiveness. | Do not present inferred traits as clinical measurement or fixed truth about a real person. | `personality_temperament` |
| HLA006 | Long-term believability depends on relationship history: trust, attachment, rupture, repair, reciprocity and boundaries. | Store relationship traces with emotion_at_write, conflict episodes, repair quality, promise tracking and salient triggers. | Dependency guards are needed for high attachment, exclusivity, separation distress and manipulative retention. | `relationship_attachment` |
| HLA007 | Agents feel continuous when they transform episodic memories into stable self-narrative and relationship summaries. | Build periodic reflections from memories: self_concept, user_relationship_story, unresolved_threads and growth notes. | Reflection must not fabricate events; every narrative claim needs memory provenance. | `autobiographical_narrative_memory` |
| HLA008 | Believable agents require coordinated observation, memory, reflection, planning and expression, not isolated prompt style. | Add a humanlike expression layer that consumes emotion, persona, memory and resource state and returns style/policy modulation. | Keep expression policy separate from core emotion math and psychological screening. | `believable_social_agents` |
| HLA009 | Clinical-adjacent modeling should be dimensional, longitudinal and non-diagnostic. | Use latent trajectories and red-flag flags only for screening/routing; never expose disease labels as bot identity. | Requires explicit non-diagnostic metadata and human-review escalation for crisis signals. | `computational_psychiatry_digital_phenotyping` |
| HLA010 | Higher humanlikeness increases anthropomorphism, attachment and dependency risks, so transparency and controls are part of the model. | Add configurable personification levels, dependency guard, reset backdoors, audit logs and simulated_state markers. | Do not use coldness, sickness, jealousy or abandonment as coercive engagement mechanics. | `safety_ethics_dependency` |
